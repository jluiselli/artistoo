How well cells can perform chemotaxis depends on several parameters.
For instance, the steeper the gradient, the easier it is for cells to perceive the direction and the larger the energy difference in the Hamiltonian.

γ is a measure of surface tension, calculated as follows: \(\gamma_{c,m}= J_{c,m}-\frac{J_{c,c}}{2}\)
γ is positive, and cells adhere, when the interfacial energy between cells and the medium is higher than the interfacial energy between cells
